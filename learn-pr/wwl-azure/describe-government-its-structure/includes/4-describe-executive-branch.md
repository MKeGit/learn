The Executive branch includes six categories of organizations.

## The White House

:::image type="content" source="../media/white-house-1200w.png" alt-text="The White House, home to the executive branch leader" border="false" lightbox="../media/white-house-1200w.png":::

## Non-cabinet-level agencies

There are numerous Independent Agencies that are non-Cabinet-level. It’s important to note that independent agencies typically have a Director or Secretary leading them.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> There are multiple roles in these agencies who are the defacto leaders for IT operations and cloud strategy who provide recommendations to the Director.

| Agency | Description |
| ------ | ----------- |
| :::image type="content" source="../media/central-intelligence-agency-logo-150.png" alt-text="The Central Intelligence Agency logo" border="false"::: | [The Central Intelligence Agency](https://www.cia.gov/) is responsible for providing security intelligence to senior U.S. policymakers. The CIA is lead by a director who is nominated by the president and confirmed by the Senate. |
| :::image type="content" source="../media/environmental-protection-agency-logo-150.png" alt-text="The Environmental Protection Agency logo" border="false"::: | [The Environmental Protection Agency](https://www.epa.gov/) is responsible for the protection of human health and the environment; providing technical assistance to support recovery planning of public health and infrastructure, such as waste water treatment plants; providing technical assistance for long-term cleanup to minimize public health threats, including environmental sampling and monitoring, site assessment, decontamination, and disposal; and providing environmental surveillance. |

## Boards

There are numerous Boards that are non-Cabinet-level. These boards have a group of individuals who govern over them and lead them.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Within Boards, there are designated roles to handle IT operations and address cloud strategy.

| Board | Description |
| ----- | ----------- |

| :::image type="content" source="../media/surface-transportation-board-logo-150.png" alt-text="The Surface Transportation Board logo" border="false"::: | [The Surface Transportation Board](https://www.stb.gov/) is responsible for broad economic regulatory oversight of railroads, including rates, service, the construction, acquisition, and abandonment of rail lines, carrier mergers, and interchange of traffic among carriers; oversight of pipeline carriers, intercity bus carriers, moving van companies, trucking companies involved in collective activities, and water carriers engaged in non-contiguous domestic trade. |

## Entities that determine the Government's cloud strategy

While many of these entities contribute in some way to the Government's cloud strategy, the most relevant agencies to the Government’s cloud strategy are:

- The Office of Management and Budget
- The Department of Defense
- The Defense Information Systems Agency
- The Department of Homeland Security
- The General Services Administration
- The National Institute of Standard and Technology
- The Federal CIO Council
